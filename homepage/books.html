I really like reading and already have read a lot of books so it is very hard for me to pick a favourite. So this is why here I present you the list books that I like a lot with my reviews on them


Alchemist by Paulo Coelho

This book is full of magical reality, nature, as well as spirituality and I fell in love with it from the first pages. This books tells a story of a shepherd who tries to find his fate and experiences all sorts of hardships. It is written in a beautiful language and shows you that while seeking your purpose in life is hard, it is very rewarding spiritually. Only incredibly brave people, who can challenge their own ways of life, listen to the nature, and eager to learn, can succeed in becoming wise alchemists and turn their lives into gold.

The count of Monte Cristo

This book tells a story of a young man who was betrayed by many people and sent to prison. There he meets an old man and undergoes massive transition and eventually escapes prison to revenge his enemies. This classic novel explores the themes of betrayal, vengeance, and the power of resilience.

"The Count of Monte Cristo" is a captivating tale that combines elements of adventure, romance, and suspense. Dumas weaves intricate plotlines and introduces a myriad of memorable characters, each with their own motives and desires. The novel not only explores themes of betrayal and revenge but also delves into the complexities of human nature and the choices we make when faced with adversity.

One hundred years of solitude by Gabriel Garcia Marquez

"One Hundred Years of Solitude" by Gabriel Garcia Marquez is a literary masterpiece that immerses readers in a richly imaginative and enchanting world. Spanning several generations of the Buendía family, the novel weaves together magical realism, history, and intricate storytelling to create a captivating tapestry of human existence.

Marquez's writing style is lyrical and evocative, drawing readers into the mythical town of Macondo, where reality and fantasy intertwine seamlessly. The story unfolds with a cast of eccentric and memorable characters, each with their own desires, flaws, and passions. From José Arcadio Buendía, the patriarch who is consumed by his pursuit of knowledge, to the ethereal and enigmatic Remedios the Beauty, the characters breathe life into the pages. As the narrative progresses, Marquez explores profound themes such as the cyclical nature of time, the intricate ties between love and death, and the inevitable solitude that accompanies the human experience. The Buendía family's recurring patterns of love, betrayal, and tragedy serve as a reflection of humanity's collective history, echoing the complexities and contradictions of the world we inhabit.

What sets "One Hundred Years of Solitude" apart is Marquez's masterful use of magical realism. He seamlessly blends the extraordinary with the ordinary, infusing the story with mythical elements that blur the boundaries of reality. Miracles, prophecies, and supernatural occurrences are presented matter-of-factly, offering a unique perspective on the nature of truth and the power of storytelling.

While the novel can be intricate and complex, Marquez's lush prose and vivid descriptions make it an immersive and rewarding reading experience. The beauty of his language lies in its ability to transport readers into a world that feels both familiar and otherworldly.

"One Hundred Years of Solitude" is a timeless work of literature that continues to resonate with readers around the world. It is a testament to the human condition, exploring the universal themes of love, loss, and the indomitable spirit of the human soul. Marquez's masterful storytelling and his ability to capture the essence of life in all its joys and sorrows make this novel an enduring classic that leaves a lasting impression on anyone who embarks on its enchanting journey.
